# Past Winners — Encoded Heuristics

Patterns that have earned their keep in real hackathon runs. Consult when scoring concepts or pressure-testing blueprints.

## The rules

1. **Physical object on camera beats UI on camera.** A hand holding a vinyl record, a photo of a real building, a paper form — any tangible artifact in the demo video lifts virality and memorability. UI screenshots blur together. (Crate Séance run, #ElevenHacks.)

2. **Your unfair-advantage corpus is more valuable than any public dataset.** A 20-year personal archive of show notes, a decade of client emails, your own field photos — these are corpora no other team can replicate. Always ask "what could *only you* index?" before reaching for Common Crawl.

3. **The non-obvious sponsor tech use is almost always: *use their tool on a corpus they'd never think to index.*** Vector search on taste (not documents). TTS on civic legislation (not marketing copy). Image gen on architectural blueprints (not product shots).

4. **Sleeper prize categories have softer competition.** Read every prize name. The one that sounds narrow or weird usually has 1/5 the submissions. Name the sleeper in the blueprint.

5. **Cache the wow moment; never demo live what you can demo pre-rendered.** Especially for async-video hackathons. The hero output ships cached; live generation is stretch.

6. **Virality math beats placement math in distribution-primary hackathons.** When social-post and virality points exceed the 1st-vs-3rd delta, optimize for the clip, not the judges. Guaranteed floor from 4 platforms often beats chasing 1st place.

7. **Shareable permalink artifacts multiply virality.** Every generated output should have its own embeddable URL with og-tags. Twitter shares become unfurled cards become reshares.

8. **Hand-curate 200 beats scraping 10,000.** Time spent on corpus quality pays back in generation quality. Scoping down the dataset is almost always the right scope cut.

9. **Email the sponsor teams the day before submission with the cut.** Sponsors champion internally what they see early. Free distribution.

10. **"This started because [personal story]"** outperforms every technical framing on stage and in captions.
